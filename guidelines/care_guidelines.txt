# Clinical Care Guidelines & Intervention Protocol Database
# Version: 2.1 - Milestone 2 Update
# Contributor: Ayush Kumar

[SECTION: GENERAL NO-SHOW PROTOCOLS]
1. Initial Contact: All patients predicted with >60% risk must receive a personal call 48 hours prior.
2. Barrier Assessment: Clinical staff should inquire about transportation, childcare, or work schedule conflicts.
3. Flexibility: Offer telehealth alternatives for patients with mobility or distance barriers.
4. Incentivization: Discuss the importance of continuity of care for long-term health outcomes.

[SECTION: DIABETES & HYPERTENSION SPECIALIZED CARE]
- Patients with hypertension exhibit 25% higher no-show rates in urban areas.
- Protocol: Ensure medication refill alignment with appointment dates.
- Intervention: "Triple Check" verification for elderly patients living in low-mobility zones.
- Education: Provide pamphlets on the impact of missed monitoring on blood sugar regulation.

[SECTION: SOCIO-ECONOMIC BARRIERS (SCHOLARSHIP PATIENTS)]
- Strategy: Prioritize morning slots for patients relying on restricted public transport schedules.
- Support: Coordinate with local social workers if financial barriers to transport are identified.
- Reminder: Use SMS text reminders with clear "Opt-out" or "Reschedule" instructions to reduce friction.

[SECTION: LEAD TIME OPTIMIZATION]
- Clinical data suggests no-show rates increase by 1.5% for every day of lead time beyond 7 days.
- Protocol: For appointments scheduled 2+ weeks out, implement a "Re-confirmation Pulse" at the 7-day mark.
- Dynamic Scheduling: Keep a "waitlist" of local low-risk patients to fill last-minute cancellations from high-risk cohorts.

[SECTION: ALCOHOLISM & MENTAL HEALTH]
- Sensitivity: Ensure reminders are delivered with patient privacy as a priority.
- Holistic Approach: Link mental health appointments with general check-ups to minimize multiple travel requirements.
- Trust Building: Assign a dedicated "Care Navigator" for chronic high-risk no-show patients.

[SECTION: SMS VERIFICATION EFFICACY]
- Evidence indicates that SMS verification reduces no-show probability by 30% on average.
- Strategy: Ensure SMS system captures "Confirmed" vs "No Response" status.
- Secondary Action: If no SMS response is received 24h prior, initiate a direct voice call.

[SECTION: PEDIATRIC & AGING INFRASTRUCTURE]
- Pediatric appointments: Target parents via personalized outreach emphasizing growth charting milestones.
- Geriatric appointments: Coordinate with family members or caregivers noted in the EMR.
- Facility Access: Verify wheelchair accessibility requirements during the 48h confirmation call.

[SECTION: ETHICAL & DATA PRIVACY CONSIDERATIONS]
- Data usage: All no-show predictions must be used for supportive intervention, never for service denial.
- Transparency: Patients should be informed that the clinic uses predictive analytics to improve service accessibility.
- Consent: Ensure all outreach channels (SMS/Call) are compliant with regional health data privacy laws.
